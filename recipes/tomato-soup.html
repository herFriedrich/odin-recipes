What to expect

Few things are as soothing and satisfying as a bowl of simple tomato soup. This version is smooth, rich, and full of deep tomato flavor with a touch of sweetness and a subtle kick of garlic and herbs. A swirl of cream or a drizzle of olive oil adds velvety texture, while crusty bread or a grilled cheese sandwich makes the perfect companion. Whether served as a light lunch, an appetizer, or a cozy dinner starter, this tomato soup is wholesome, nourishing, and timeless — a true kitchen staple made with love.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or butter
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) whole peeled tomatoes or crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil or oregano (optional)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • ¼ cup heavy cream or milk (optional, for creaminess)
  • Optional: fresh basil or parsley for garnish

How...

  • Step 1: Sauté the Aromatics
    1. Heat olive oil or butter in a large pot over medium heat.
    2. Add chopped onion and cook until soft and translucent, about 5–7 minutes.
    3. Add minced garlic and sauté for another 1 minute until fragrant.

  • Step 2: Simmer the Soup
    1. Pour in the canned tomatoes (with juices) and the broth.
    2. Stir in sugar, dried herbs, salt, and pepper to taste.
    3. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer uncovered for 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally.

  • Step 3: Blend and Finish
    1. Use an immersion blender to puree the soup until smooth directly in the pot, or transfer to a blender in batches (be careful with hot liquid).
    2. Stir in cream or milk if using, and adjust seasoning as needed.
    3. Reheat gently if necessary.

  • Step 4: Serve
    1. Ladle into bowls, garnish with fresh herbs or a drizzle of cream.
    2. Serve warm with crusty bread, grilled cheese, or a side salad.
