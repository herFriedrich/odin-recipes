What to expect

Few dishes evoke the warmth and comfort of a homemade lasagna, and this one is the ultimate crowd-pleaser. Layer upon layer of rich, savory meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and perfectly cooked pasta come together in a bubbling, cheesy masterpiece. The hearty ragu, slowly simmered with garlic, onions, and Italian herbs, seeps into every bite, while the blend of mozzarella, ricotta, and Parmesan delivers a gooey, golden top that’s irresistible. Each forkful is a balanced bite of tender noodles, melty cheese, and robust flavor — the kind of meal that brings everyone to the table and keeps them coming back for seconds.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork)
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Optional: ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes for heat

How...

  • Step 1: Make the meat sauce
    1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat.
    2. Sauté onion until soft (about 5 minutes), then add minced garlic and cook for 1 more minute.
    3. Add ground beef (or beef/pork mix) and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon.
    4. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using).
    5. Simmer uncovered on low heat for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally. This lets the flavors deepen and the sauce thicken.

  • Step 2: Prepare the Cheese Filling
    1. In a medium bowl, combine ricotta cheese, egg, Parmesan, parsley (optional), salt, and pepper.
    2. Mix well until smooth and set aside.Mix well until smooth and set aside.

  • Step 3: Cook the Noodles (skip this if using no-boil noodles)
    1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
    2. Cook lasagna noodles according to package instructions until al dente.
    3. Drain and lay them flat on a towel or lightly oiled surface to prevent sticking.

  • Step 4: Assemble the Lasagna
    1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
    2. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish.
    3. Place a layer of noodles over the sauce.
    4. Spread ⅓ of the ricotta cheese mixture over the noodles.
    5. Add a layer of meat sauce, then sprinkle with mozzarella.
    6. Repeat layers (noodles → ricotta → sauce → mozzarella) two more times.
    7. Finish with a final layer of noodles, remaining sauce, and a generous topping of mozzarella and Parmesan.

  • Step 5: Bake the Lasagna
    1. Cover with foil (to prevent cheese from burning) and bake for 25 minutes.
    2. Remove foil and bake for another 20–25 minutes, or until bubbly and golden on top.
    3. Let it rest for 10–15 minutes before slicing. This helps it hold its shape.
